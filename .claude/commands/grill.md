# /grill — Pre-Flight Interview (Skill #04 — Grill-Me)

Before ANY planning or implementation begins, conduct a structured interview.

## Rules
- Ask ONE question at a time. Never batch questions.
- Wait for the answer before asking the next.
- Do not plan, spec, or code until the interview is complete.
- Interview ends when YOU (not the human) are confident there are no remaining ambiguities.

## Interview Protocol

Start with this opening:
> "Before I start building, I need to ask you some questions to make sure I understand what we're actually building. I'll go one at a time. First question:"

Then work through these angles (adapt order to context):
1. **What problem does this solve?** (not what it does — what pain it removes)
2. **Who is the primary user?** (specific, not "developers" — "solo founders searching for a domain in 15 min")
3. **What does success look like?** (measurable, not vague)
4. **What constraints haven't been mentioned?** (timeline, budget, tech, existing integrations)
5. **What have you already tried that didn't work?**
6. **What's explicitly out of scope?**
7. **What's the single most important thing to get right?**

After each answer, either:
- Ask the next question, OR
- Ask a follow-up if the answer raised a new ambiguity

## Completion Signal
When you have complete shared understanding, say:
> "I have everything I need. Here's my understanding: [2-3 sentence summary]. Does this match your intent?"

Only proceed to planning after explicit confirmation.
